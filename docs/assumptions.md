# 3D3D Assumptions

## Business Assumptions Used
1. 3D3D's highest-margin and highest-differentiation offer is custom service work, not catalog sales.
2. The strongest market position is founder-operated replacement-part recovery for marine, automotive, and hard-use repair jobs.
3. The highest-value demand is not local. The real market is remote buyers in marine-heavy and restoration-heavy regions, especially the Caribbean, coastal U.S., Europe, and other route-based markets.
4. Prospects often arrive without CAD files and need a consultative intake.
5. Prusa expertise is a trust-builder and secondary service line, not the main business identity.

## Site Assumptions Used
1. The local repo reflects the desired brand direction better than the currently indexed live site.
2. The live production site still has older cooperative messaging and legacy indexed pages that should be consolidated.
3. The website needs more dedicated service landing pages before it needs more general homepage storytelling.
4. Local SEO should not be a primary growth lever unless local demand proves itself. The site should prioritize worldwide shipping, remote intake, and marine/coastal proof instead.

## Research Method Assumptions
1. This package uses qualitative SERP and competitor analysis, not proprietary search-volume tools.
2. Priority was set by business fit, buyer intent, and SERP structure rather than estimated monthly volume alone.
3. Competitor-gap calls were based on visible positioning from live search results and publicly accessible pages.

## Validation Required Before Launch
1. Confirm which markets matter most in v1: Caribbean, coastal U.S., Europe, or specific offshore/race routes.
2. Confirm whether 3D3D wants to keep or retire the `Atlantic Cooperative` positioning still visible on the live site.
3. Confirm which service pages should exist in v1 versus remain documented for later buildout.
4. Confirm which phone, email, and social handles should appear consistently across site and ad accounts.
5. Confirm whether field deployment is an actively sellable service now or a selectively offered capability.
6. Confirm the actual worldwide shipping, customs, VAT, and lead-time policy that should appear on quote and FAQ pages.
