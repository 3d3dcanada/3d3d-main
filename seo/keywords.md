# 3D3D Keyword System

## Search Thesis
3D3D should not optimize like a neighborhood print shop. The highest-value demand is remote, urgent, and niche:

- Boat and yacht owners who need replacement parts fast
- Offshore crews, race programs, and refit teams dealing with hard-use failures
- Automotive restorers looking for discontinued plastic parts
- Buyers with broken samples instead of clean CAD
- Worldwide customers who care more about capability, fit, and shipping than local proximity

This keyword map is built from the current repo, the live indexed site, and live SERP/competitor patterns from Solvit3D, Nautica 3D, Custom Boat Parts 3D, 3DCarParts.org, and broader marine-repair search behavior.

## Competitor Keyword Gap Analysis
| Competitor / SERP Pattern | Terms They Lean On | Current 3D3D Gap | Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Nautica 3D | `hard to find`, `custom made`, `out-of-production`, marine replacement parts | 3D3D has better founder-story potential, but not enough exact-match marine replacement language | Build a marine page with hard-to-find, obsolete, and replacement-part phrasing plus shipping/process clarity |
| Custom Boat Parts 3D | `classic boat parts`, `no longer available`, owner-led restoration angle | 3D3D has strong offshore credibility but does not yet package it into boat-owner language | Add marine case studies, before/after proof, and yacht-owner-focused FAQ |
| Solvit3D | `reverse engineering`, `digital warehousing`, global support, engineering service language | Reverse engineering exists conceptually but is under-structured in the current package | Launch `/reverse-engineering` with sample intake, model/test-fit workflow, and repeat-order language |
| 3DCarParts.org | `hard-to-find car parts`, `verified fit`, `worldwide delivery`, safety FAQ | 3D3D has the mechanic angle, but not enough explicit automotive safety-scope language | Strengthen automotive page with fitment notes, safety guardrails, and non-safety-critical FAQ |
| Generic local print shops | `local 3d printing`, `rapid prototyping`, generic menus | 3D3D should not compete as a commodity local shop | Remove local-first SEO from the strategy and lead with replacement-part recovery, marine durability, and remote capability |

## Keyword Gaps to Close in the Next 90 Days
1. Create dedicated service pages for marine, automotive, reverse engineering, field deployment, and Prusa consulting.
2. Publish market-specific landing pages only where real proof exists: Caribbean, coastal U.S., Europe, or specific race/marina routes.
3. Convert existing materials content into an interlinked materials cluster around marine, automotive, and outdoor durability.
4. Add FAQ blocks using exact-match phrasing around broken samples, worldwide shipping, obsolete yacht parts, and classic car parts.
5. Standardize one primary positioning line across metadata, YouTube, and social bios: `Custom 3D printed replacement parts for marine, automotive, and hard-to-source repair jobs worldwide.`
