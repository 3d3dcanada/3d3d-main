# 3D3D Strategic Decisions

## Core Decisions Made
1. Prioritized service SEO over shop SEO because service intent is higher value and more defensible.
2. Centered the brand around replacement parts, reverse engineering, and hard-use environments instead of generic “3D printing.”
3. Recommended dedicated marine, automotive, reverse-engineering, field-deployment, and Prusa consulting landing pages.
4. Treated worldwide marine and remote-buyer demand as the major opportunity because local New Brunswick demand is not the commercial target.
5. Used direct, opinionated messaging because that matches both the founder voice and the strongest-performing content patterns in this niche.
6. Built the content engine around real jobs, material decisions, and failure analysis rather than inspiration or lifestyle posting.
7. Positioned field deployment as a premium differentiator, not as default messaging on every page.
8. Kept shop products as a secondary commerce layer; they support credibility and long-tail traffic, but they should not dilute the main service story.
9. Included brand-safe edge and controversy because neutral, generic content will not cut through in the current feed environment.
10. Structured every platform around one conversion path: quote, consult, or deployment inquiry.

## Non-Decisions Left Open
1. Whether `/prusa` becomes `/prusa-consulting` immediately or later.
2. Whether market-route landing pages launch with unique proof-led copy or reuse modular service blocks first.
3. Whether any local-business profiles should remain part of the strategy at all.
4. Whether shop products should be separated into their own paid-social funnel later.
