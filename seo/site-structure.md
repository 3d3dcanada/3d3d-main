# 3D3D SEO Page Architecture

## Recommended Architecture
The site should be organized around a clear revenue hierarchy:

1. Homepage as the authority and routing page.
2. Service pillars for highest-intent niches.
3. Supporting market-route landing pages for global demand.
4. Blog hub for topical authority and internal link support.
5. Shop/catalog as a secondary commerce engine, not the primary homepage story.

## SEO FAQ Set
Use these on service pages and the quote page with FAQ schema.

### Marine
**Can 3D printed parts survive on a boat?**  
Yes, when the material is chosen for UV, saltwater, heat, and mechanical load. ASA, PETG-CF, PP-based compounds, PA11 CF, and TPU all have marine use cases; PLA usually does not.

**Can you remake a broken boat part if I only have the damaged piece?**  
Yes. A broken sample, photos, and rough dimensions are often enough to start a reverse-engineering job.

### Automotive
**Can you reproduce discontinued car interior parts?**  
Yes. Interior trim, clips, knobs, bezels, covers, and other non-safety-critical plastic parts are strong candidates for reverse engineering and replacement.

**Can you 3D print engine-bay parts?**  
Some of them. Heat, oil, fuel exposure, and vibration determine the material and whether a part is suitable for printing. 3D3D evaluates that before production.

### General
**Do I need a finished 3D model to get a quote?**  
No. Files help, but 3D3D also works from samples, photos, drawings, and problem descriptions.

**Can you work with buyers outside your home base?**  
Yes. 3D3D is built for remote intake. Buyers can start with photos, files, broken samples, and shipping details from wherever the part problem exists.

**Can you come on site?**  
Yes. For the right job, 3D3D offers field deployment and on-site setup support.

## Technical SEO Recommendations

### Highest priority
1. Consolidate legacy live-site pages and old indexed cooperative URLs into the current positioning with 301 redirects.
2. Resolve duplicate or low-value indexed URLs, including legacy `/home`-style routes and query-parameter pages like `?mailpoet_page=captcha`.
3. Ship dedicated service pillar pages before adding more broad blog volume.
4. Add LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage, and BreadcrumbList schema where relevant.

### Page speed
- Serve hero imagery in next-gen formats and ensure aggressive compression for large sailing/workshop media.
- Use poster images and lazy loading for non-critical video.
- Avoid autoplay video above the fold on mobile if it harms LCP; prefer static hero fallback for slower connections.
- Preload only the primary hero image and first font files actually used above the fold.

### Indexing
- Keep legal pages indexable but deprioritized in internal linking.
- Add `noindex` to thin thank-you states, file-upload intermediates, and any duplicate utility states.
- Ensure shop filters and sort states do not create crawl waste.

### Sitemap strategy
- Primary XML sitemap index with separate sitemaps for pages, blog posts, and shop products once page count grows.
- Include `lastmod` for blog posts and core services.
- Submit the canonical production sitemap in Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.

### Canonical rules
- Self-canonicalize all canonical pages.
- Canonicalize query-string variants back to the clean URL.
- If `/prusa` is repositioned to consulting, redirect to `/prusa-consulting`.
- If live production still exposes older cooperative pages, choose one brand architecture and canonicalize aggressively.

### International / service-area SEO
- Stop optimizing around local city pages unless a market proves it can convert.
- Use the same service-area language across site, schema, social bios, and outreach: worldwide shipping, remote intake, marine/coastal focus.
- Build market-route pages only where 3D3D has genuine proof, logistics clarity, or event/route relevance.
- Add FAQ copy covering shipping, lead times, customs, and what buyers need to send for remote reverse-engineering jobs.
