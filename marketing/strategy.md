# 3D3D Marketing Strategy

## Positioning Core
**Category**  
Founder-operated custom fabrication and reverse-engineering studio specializing in hard-to-source plastic parts.

**Primary audience**
- Boat owners, yacht crews, refit teams, and offshore/race programs with broken or impossible-to-source parts
- Remote buyers in the Caribbean, coastal U.S., Europe, and other marine-heavy markets
- Classic car owners, restorers, and motorsport builders
- Small operators who need a functional part, not a design lecture
- Buyers considering Prusa equipment or needing setup help

**Primary promise**  
If the part is broken, discontinued, or never existed in the first place, 3D3D can figure out a practical replacement path.

**Primary differentiators**
- Mechanic-led automotive credibility
- Offshore and marine use-case credibility
- Reverse engineering from imperfect inputs
- Material selection based on environment, not generic printer-shop menus
- Field deployment / on-site fabrication option

## Offer Positioning
### Offer 1: Replacement-Part Recovery
Best for broken, discontinued, or unavailable plastic parts. Position this as the flagship offer.

**Positioning line**  
When sourcing dies, 3D3D becomes the supply chain.

### Offer 2: Environment-Specific Fabrication
Marine, automotive, motorsport, and outdoor applications.

**Positioning line**  
Built for the load case and environment the part actually lives in.

### Offer 3: Reverse Engineering + Test Fit
Best for incomplete samples and uncertain geometry.

**Positioning line**  
From damaged sample to working replacement.

### Offer 4: Field Deployment
Best for urgent or location-bound jobs.

**Positioning line**  
If the problem cannot be shipped, 3D3D can come to it.

### Offer 5: Prusa Advisory
Best for easier entry-point revenue and trust building.

**Positioning line**  
Talk to someone who has already made the expensive mistakes.

### Trust-Building Statements
- Founder-operated. The person quoting the job understands the job.
- Built around real marine and automotive use cases, not tabletop-only assumptions.
- Works from files, photos, and damaged samples.
- Material recommendations are tied to load, heat, UV, moisture, and chemicals.
- Remote-first process built for worldwide buyers, not walk-in traffic.
- Quote-first process designed for real projects, not slicer hobbyists.
- Reverse engineering available for broken and discontinued parts.
- Practical advice on what should not be printed is part of the service.

### Objection Handling Lines
- `I do not have CAD files.`  
  You do not need them. A broken sample, photos, or rough dimensions are enough to start many jobs.

- `I only need one part.`  
  One-off replacement work is part of the business model, not an inconvenience.

- `I am not sure 3D printing is strong enough.`  
  Some jobs are a strong fit, some are not. 3D3D evaluates the environment and material before taking the work forward.

- `I am worried the part will not fit.`  
  Reverse engineering and test-fit iteration are built into the process for jobs where fitment matters.

- `I need this fast.`  
  The quote flow is built for urgent intake, and field deployment is available when shipping is the bottleneck.

- `I do not know what material I need.`  
  That is the point of the consultative intake. 3D3D specs the material to the failure mode.

## Platform Strategy

### Facebook
**Role**  
Demand capture from owner communities and buyer groups: marinas, yacht owners, offshore/race circles, restoration communities, and older buyers who still transact via Facebook first.

**Content style**
- Story-led posts with proof photos
- Clear before/after or problem/solution framing
- Comments-friendly local angle
- More context, more explanation, more direct service offers

**Conversion approach**
- Push service inquiries into Messenger, WhatsApp, or `/quote`
- Use case studies and FAQ posts to remove doubt
- Retarget page visitors with proof-heavy ads

### Instagram
**Role**  
Brand desirability and attention engine. Show the process, textures, machines, parts, marine environments, and founder credibility.

**Content style**
- Reels-first, tight hooks, crisp overlays
- Carousel proof breakdowns
- Workshop and offshore visuals
- Captions shorter than Facebook, but sharper

**Conversion approach**
- Reels and stories drive DM conversations
- Link-in-bio pushes to quote page or service page
- Use pinned highlights: `Marine`, `Auto`, `Parts`, `Prusa`, `Quote`

### YouTube
**Role**  
Authority compounding. This is where 3D3D proves expertise, explains materials, and turns story into trust at a scale social feeds cannot.

**Content style**
- Strong first 20 seconds
- Direct point of view, no generic intro filler
- Case-study and comparison content
- Founder voice over polished corporate tone

**Conversion approach**
- Long-form builds authority and ranks in search
- Shorts feed top-of-funnel attention
- Every video points viewers to one next step: quote, materials page, or consulting call

## Messaging Guardrails
- Lead with the part problem, not the printer.
- Use real environments: saltwater, heat, UV, oil, vibration, fitment.
- Avoid generic “innovation” language.
- Keep the founder voice direct, blunt, and competent.
- Always convert curiosity into one next step: quote, send photos, or book a consult.
