# 3D3D Creative Generation Prompts

Use these prompts for a downstream image-generation agent. Keep the outputs grounded in real fabrication, real environments, and real material surfaces.

## Social Post Graphics

### SPG-01
Create a square social graphic for 3D3D showing a broken plastic replacement part on the left and a rebuilt functional version on the right. Use a dark graphite industrial background, a sharp vertical split, teal accent glow around the rebuilt part, and a subtle coral highlight tracing the crack on the broken piece. Add bold condensed headline text: `DISCONTINUED?` on the top line and `REBUILD IT.` beneath it. Add a small footer line in a monospaced technical style: `Reverse-engineered by 3D3D`. Make it feel real, mechanical, and high-competence, not sci-fi.

### SPG-02
Create a square marine-warning graphic using a close-up photo-real boat hardware or deck fitting background with salt spray texture and a dark overlay. Add bold white text in the upper left: `STOP PRINTING BOAT PARTS IN PLA`, with `PLA` highlighted in coral and a thin teal underline. Add a smaller secondary line: `Material choice is the job.` Keep the visual style premium, rugged, and believable.

### SPG-03
Create a three-panel square graphic for 3D3D. Panel one: broken part sample. Panel two: rough measurements with calipers. Panel three: finished replacement print. Use a graphite outer frame and light bone internal panels. Add headline text: `NO CAD FILE?` and beneath it `Still workable.` Add a footer in small type: `Photos. Sample. Measurements.` The whole graphic should feel clear, practical, and founder-operated.

### SPG-04
Create a square technical graphic with a printed replacement part over an engine-bay background. Use subtle callout lines pointing to stress areas. Add stacked bold words: `HEAT.` `VIBRATION.` `FITMENT.` and a footer line `A mechanic looks differently.` Use graphite, teal, and coral. Make the part the hero and keep the overlay sharp and readable.

### SPG-05
Create a square checklist graphic with a clean utility look. Left side is a bone-colored checklist card with the headline `SEND THESE 4 PHOTOS` and four short bullets. Right side is a realistic phone mockup showing a quote request page. Use teal check marks, graphite text, and a small footer line `Start at 3d3d.ca/quote`. Designed to be saved and shared.

### SPG-06
Create a square split-identity graphic. Top left shows a marine hardware close-up. Bottom right shows classic-car trim or bracket detail. Use a diagonal split with teal on the marine side and coral on the automotive side. Add headline text: `BOAT PARTS FAIL DIFFERENTLY` and subhead `So do car parts.` Keep it clean, high-contrast, and strongly niche-specific.

### SPG-07
Create a cinematic square graphic showing road cases, tools, and portable printing gear in the foreground with a marina or event environment in the background. Add bold headline text: `IF THE PROBLEM CAN'T SHIP` and subhead `Bring the fabricator.` Use a dark palette with teal line accents and white text. Make it feel mobile, capable, and serious.

### SPG-08
Create a square infographic divided into four quadrants labeled `UV`, `Heat`, `Chemicals`, and `Flex`. Use teal to indicate fit zones and coral to indicate risk zones. Add a bold top headline: `CHOOSE FOR FAILURE MODE` and a footer line `Not by default filament.` The style should feel like an engineering cheat sheet, not a classroom poster.

### SPG-09
Create a square graphic showing a classic car dashboard or trim area with a clearly missing part circled in coral. Use mostly desaturated tones with a teal footer bar. Add headline text: `ONE MISSING PART` and smaller line `can stop the whole restoration`. Make the frustration immediate and relatable.

### SPG-10
Create a square founder-credibility graphic with a real workbench portrait: hands, calipers, printed parts, notebook, and workshop texture. Add headline `FOUNDER-OPERATED` and subhead `The person quoting the job understands the job.` Use muted photography, teal accents, and soft bone text blocks. Avoid startup clichés.

## Ad Creatives

### ADC-01
Create a square paid-social ad showing a broken plastic part in close-up against a dark graphite background. Overlay a large headline box with `BROKEN PART?` on the first line and `START HERE.` on the second. Add a bright teal CTA-style button reading `Request a Quote`. Add a coral accent along the fracture line. Clean, mobile-first, conversion-focused.

### ADC-02
Create a square marine-service ad using a close-up of real boat hardware or deck equipment. Place a bold teal headline band across the image with text: `BOAT PARTS` and beneath it `BUILT FOR UV, SALT, VIBRATION`. Add a small coral supporting line. The ad should feel rugged, expert, and specific to marine work.

### ADC-03
Create a square automotive restoration ad with a classic interior trim or small bracket close-up, plus a small before/after inset. Add headline text: `DEALER SAYS DISCONTINUED` and below it `3D3D SAYS SEND IT`. Use graphite, bone, and coral urgency accents. Make it direct and unmistakably aimed at restorers.

### ADC-04
Create a square process ad with four evenly spaced panels: broken sample, caliper measurement, CAD model view, finished printed part. Add one strong headline across the top: `BROKEN SAMPLE TO WORKING PART`. Use a light bone base with teal step numbers and graphite body labels. Educational but clearly commercial.

### ADC-05
Create a square advisory ad about Prusa printer consulting. Show a clean printer silhouette with sticky-note style annotations around it. Add headline: `THINKING ABOUT A PRUSA?` and subhead `Talk before you buy.` Use graphite background, teal notes, and bone type. Make it feel like expert buyer guidance, not hype.

## Story Formats

### ST-01
Create a vertical story graphic showing a single dramatic part photo with dark overlay and the question `Would you print this?` at the top. Leave room for poll buttons in the lower third. Use teal accent marks and keep the design interactive and simple.

### ST-02
Create a vertical checklist story with the headline `4 photos for a faster quote`, four numbered steps, a bone background, graphite text, and teal check icons. Make it minimal, clean, and useful enough to screenshot.

### ST-03
Create a vertical before/after proof story with the broken part on the top half and the finished printed part on the bottom half. Use a coral `Before` tag and a teal `After` tag. Keep the photography tight and high contrast.

### ST-04
Create a vertical founder-point-of-view cover frame using workshop footage or a workbench portrait. Add the prompt text `What killed the original part?` in bold and leave safe-space for subtitles at the bottom. Teal captions, dark overlay, direct and authoritative.

### ST-05
Create a vertical CTA story showing a realistic phone mockup of the 3D3D quote page centered on a graphite background with arrows and micro-copy reading `Send the file.` `Send the photo.` `Start here.` Use a teal button-style element at the bottom.

## YouTube Thumbnails

### YT-01
Create a YouTube thumbnail with a large broken plastic part in the foreground and a smaller finished rebuilt version as an inset. Use bold text: `DISCONTINUED?` and `REBUILT`. Graphite background, teal highlight, coral crack detail. Make the part the hero.

### YT-02
Create a YouTube thumbnail for a marine materials video showing rugged boat hardware and a coral X over a PLA spool. Add the text `STOP USING PLA` in large, high-contrast type. The image should feel opinionated, practical, and marine-specific.

### YT-03
Create a YouTube thumbnail showing a broken sample in hand, calipers, and a rough sketch. Add bold text: `NO CAD?` Use documentary realism, bone text on graphite, teal underline, and minimal clutter.

### YT-04
Create a YouTube thumbnail showing a printed automotive part over an engine-bay background with a founder figure or pointing hand. Add text `MECHANIC VIEW`. Use coral arrows and teal footer accents. The thumbnail should feel like earned authority, not generic creator-face content.

### YT-05
Create a YouTube thumbnail about field deployment showing portable printer gear, road cases, and a marina or event background. Add text: `I BROUGHT THE PRINTER`. Use dark tones, teal headline text, and bone supporting accents. Emphasize movement and location.
