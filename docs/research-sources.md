# 3D3D Research Sources

## Why This File Exists
This package is intentionally qualitative and market-structure driven. The goal is not to mimic broad-volume local SEO. The goal is to identify the kinds of pages, phrases, and proof patterns that already win in replacement-part, reverse-engineering, marine, and restoration search.

## Primary Sources Reviewed

### 1. 3D3D live site
- URL: `https://3d3d.ca/`
- What it informed:
  - The live site already contains stronger marine, founder-story, and real-world credibility than a generic print-shop brand.
  - The indexed footprint still needs tighter consolidation and clearer service architecture.

### 2. Legacy indexed utility URL
- URL: `https://3d3d.ca/?mailpoet_page=captcha`
- What it informed:
  - Technical SEO cleanup is still necessary before scaling content volume.
  - Query-string and low-value utility pages should not stay indexable.

### 3. Solvit3D reverse engineering
- URL: `https://www.solvit3d.com/reverse-engineering`
- What it informed:
  - Mature service pages frame reverse engineering as a structured business process, not a side capability.
  - The package should make intake, modeling, iteration, and repeatability more explicit.

### 4. Nautica 3D
- URL: `https://www.nautica3d.com/`
- What it informed:
  - Marine buyers respond to hard-to-find, custom-made, and obsolete-part language.
  - Boat-owner wording is more commercially useful than generic fabrication wording.

### 5. Custom Boat Parts 3D
- URL: `https://customboatparts3d.com/`
- What it informed:
  - Owner-led, marine-specific messaging is credible when paired with real examples.
  - There is clear demand for no-longer-available boat parts and restoration-adjacent marine replacement work.

### 6. 3DCarParts.org
- URL: `https://3dcarparts.org/`
- What it informed:
  - Automotive buyers respond to discontinued-part language, fitment concerns, and safety-scope clarity.
  - Automotive service pages should separate non-safety-critical use cases from anything that should be declined.

### 7. As-Built reverse engineering
- URL: `https://www.as-built.co/services/reverse-engineering`
- What it informed:
  - Reverse-engineering pages perform better when they define deliverables, not just capabilities.
  - The package should keep emphasizing model rebuild, fit validation, and repeat-order potential.

## Strategic Conclusions
1. 3D3D should compete as a specialist replacement-part operator, not a general local print shop.
2. Marine and offshore replacement parts are the clearest path to distinctive SEO and conversion positioning.
3. Reverse engineering deserves first-class page architecture, metadata, schema, and CTA treatment.
4. Worldwide shipping and remote intake should be explicit because the right buyer is not searching for a New Brunswick vendor.
5. Proof, FAQ clarity, and service-page structure will likely outperform broad blog volume in the next phase.
